📄 Research Paper: Factory-to-Customer Shipping Route Efficiency Analysis
Organization: Nassau Candy Distributor

Domain: Supply Chain & Logistics Analytics

Tech Stack: Python, Jupyter Notebooks, Pandas, Streamlit

1. Executive Summary
Nassau Candy Distributor operates as a national distributor, shipping products from multiple factories to customers across various US regions. This project transforms raw order and shipment data into route-level operational intelligence. By engineering key metrics like Shipping Lead Time and Delay Frequency, this analysis identifies geographic bottlenecks, evaluates ship mode performance, and provides actionable, data-driven recommendations to improve nationwide delivery reliability and reduce operational costs.

2. Background & Problem Statement
Despite having rich order and shipment data, logistics decisions were previously made without route-level efficiency intelligence. This lack of visibility meant logistics optimization was reactive rather than data-driven.

Key challenges addressed in this research:

Identifying which factory-to-customer routes are consistently efficient and which experience frequent delays.

Understanding how shipping performance varies by region, state, and ship mode.

Locating geographical operational bottlenecks where high volume meets poor performance.

3. Data Architecture & Preprocessing
The analysis integrates customer order data with specific factory mapping based on product divisions (e.g., matching "Laffy Taffy" to the Sugar Shack factory and "Wonka Bars" to Lot's O' Nuts or Wicked Choccy's).

Data Cleaning & Feature Engineering (01_data_cleaning_validation.ipynb):

Validation: Standardized geographic fields, validated date formats, and removed invalid/negative lead times.

Feature Creation: Calculated the primary KPI, Shipping Lead Time (Ship Date - Order Date).

Categorization: Grouped shipments by Ship Mode and defined distinct routes (Factory → Customer State/Region).

4. Analytical Methodology & EDA
The research was broken down into targeted exploratory data analysis phases to isolate different variables affecting shipping efficiency:

A. Route Efficiency Benchmarking (02_route_efficiency_analysis.ipynb)

Aggregated total shipments and average shipping lead times for every Factory → Customer route.

Calculated lead time variability to rank routes from fastest to slowest, clearly defining the Top 10 most efficient and Bottom 10 least efficient logistical paths.

B. Geographic Bottleneck Analysis (03_geographic_bottleneck_analysis.ipynb)

Mapped average lead times against regional shipment volumes.

Identified specific congestion-prone states/regions where high order volumes correlate with sustained delivery delays.

C. Ship Mode & Delay Patterns (04_ship_mode_performance_analysis.ipynb & 05_shipping_delay_pattern_analysis.ipynb)

Evaluated the cost-time tradeoffs between Standard and Expedited shipping methods.

Analyzed "Delay Frequency" (the percentage of shipments exceeding target thresholds) to find systemic failures in specific shipping modes.

6. Strategic Recommendations
Based on the Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) established in 06_logistics_kpi_analysis.ipynb, the following actions are recommended:

Reroute or Reallocate Inventory: Shift high-volume product distributions away from the identified bottleneck regions or utilize secondary distribution centers closer to highly congested states.

Optimize Ship Mode Offerings: Reevaluate expedited shipping guarantees in regions where route efficiency scores remain low regardless of the chosen shipping method.

Proactive Delay Management: Utilize the historical delay patterns to set more accurate delivery expectations for customers in historically slow regions, directly improving customer satisfaction.

7. Deliverables & Web Application
To ensure these insights remain accessible and actionable for stakeholders, the static research was translated into a live analytical tool:

Processed Datasets: Cleaned KPI datasets (route_efficiency_data.csv, geographic_bottleneck_analysis.csv, etc.) available in the data/processed/ directory.

Streamlit Dashboard (app.py): A live web application allowing users to filter data by date range, region, and ship mode. It features a Route Performance Leaderboard, a US Shipping Heatmap, and order-level drill-down capabilities.
